# Pet Health Advice

This skill provides comprehensive pet health advice, care tips, and medical information for various pet types. It covers nutrition, exercise, preventive care, common illnesses, and emergency situations.

## When to Use

Use this skill when you need information about:

- Pet nutrition and feeding guidelines
- Exercise and activity requirements
- Preventive care and vaccinations
- Common illnesses and symptoms
- Emergency warning signs
- Grooming and hygiene
- Behavioral issues
- Senior pet care

## Supported Pet Types

- **Dogs** - All breeds and sizes
- **Cats** - Indoor and outdoor cats
- **Birds** - Common pet birds
- **Rabbits** - Domestic rabbits
- **Hamsters** - Small rodents
- **Fish** - Aquarium fish
- **Reptiles** - Common pet reptiles

## Topics Covered

### 1. Nutrition

Proper nutrition is essential for your pet's health and longevity.

#### Dogs

**Daily Feeding Requirements:**

- **Adult Dogs**: Feed 2-3 times per day
- **Puppies**: Feed 3-4 times per day
- **Senior Dogs**: May need smaller, more frequent meals

**Essential Nutrients:**

1. **Protein**: High-quality animal protein (25-30% of diet)
2. **Fats**: Essential fatty acids for skin and coat health
3. **Carbohydrates**: Whole grains and vegetables
4. **Vitamins & Minerals**: Balanced commercial food usually provides these

**Foods to Avoid:**

- Chocolate
- Grapes and raisins
- Onions and garlic
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
- Alcohol
- Caffeine

**Feeding Tips:**

- Measure food portions accurately
- Provide fresh water at all times
- Avoid table scraps
- Consider age, size, and activity level when determining portions

#### Cats

**Feeding Requirements:**

- **Adult Cats**: 2-3 meals per day
- **Kittens**: 4-6 small meals per day
- **Senior Cats**: May need special diets

**Essential Nutrients:**

1. **High Protein**: Cats are obligate carnivores (30-40% protein)
2. **Taurine**: Essential amino acid (must be in diet)
3. **Fats**: For energy and healthy skin
4. **Water**: Cats need plenty of fresh water

**Foods to Avoid:**

- Onions and garlic
- Chocolate
- Alcohol
- Caffeine
- Raw fish (can cause thiamine deficiency)
- Milk (many cats are lactose intolerant)

**Special Considerations:**

- Wet food helps with hydration
- Dry food helps with dental health
- Consider a mix of both

### 2. Exercise

Regular exercise is crucial for maintaining your pet's physical and mental health.

#### Dogs

**Daily Exercise Requirements:**

- **Small Dogs**: 30-60 minutes per day
- **Medium Dogs**: 1-2 hours per day
- **Large Dogs**: 2+ hours per day
- **Puppies**: Short, frequent sessions (5-10 minutes multiple times)

**Types of Exercise:**

1. **Walking**: Daily walks are essential
2. **Running**: For high-energy breeds
3. **Playing**: Fetch, tug-of-war, interactive toys
4. **Swimming**: Great low-impact exercise
5. **Mental Stimulation**: Puzzle toys, training sessions

**Exercise Benefits:**

- Maintains healthy weight
- Prevents behavioral issues
- Strengthens muscles and joints
- Improves cardiovascular health
- Reduces anxiety and stress

#### Cats

**Daily Activity Requirements:**

- **Indoor Cats**: 20-30 minutes of active play
- **Outdoor Cats**: Natural exercise, but still benefit from play
- **Kittens**: Multiple short play sessions throughout the day

**Types of Exercise:**

1. **Interactive Play**: Wand toys, laser pointers (use carefully)
2. **Climbing**: Cat trees and shelves
3. **Hunting Games**: Hide treats, puzzle feeders
4. **Chasing**: Balls, toy mice
5. **Mental Stimulation**: Puzzle toys, training

**Exercise Benefits:**

- Prevents obesity
- Reduces stress and anxiety
- Prevents destructive behavior
- Strengthens muscles
- Improves overall health

### 3. Preventive Care

Regular preventive care helps catch health issues early and keeps your pet healthy.

#### Dogs

**Regular Veterinary Visits:**

- **Puppies**: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
- **Adults**: Annual checkups
- **Seniors**: Every 6 months

**Vaccinations:**

- Core vaccines: DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)
- Rabies (required by law)
- Non-core vaccines based on lifestyle and location

**Parasite Prevention:**

- **Fleas & Ticks**: Monthly preventatives
- **Heartworm**: Monthly prevention (year-round recommended)
- **Intestinal Parasites**: Regular fecal exams and deworming

**Dental Care:**

- Daily tooth brushing
- Dental chews and toys
- Annual dental cleanings
- Watch for signs of dental disease

**Grooming:**

- Regular brushing
- Nail trimming
- Ear cleaning
- Bathing as needed

#### Cats

**Regular Veterinary Visits:**

- **Kittens**: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
- **Adults**: Annual checkups
- **Seniors**: Every 6 months

**Vaccinations:**

- Core vaccines: FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia)
- Rabies (required by law)
- FeLV (Feline Leukemia) for at-risk cats

**Parasite Prevention:**

- **Fleas**: Monthly preventatives
- **Ticks**: Monthly preventatives (if outdoor)
- **Heartworm**: Monthly prevention
- **Intestinal Parasites**: Regular fecal exams

**Dental Care:**

- Daily tooth brushing (if possible)
- Dental treats and toys
- Annual dental cleanings
- Watch for signs of dental disease

**Grooming:**

- Regular brushing (especially long-haired cats)
- Nail trimming
- Check ears regularly

### 4. Common Illnesses

#### Dogs

**Canine Parvovirus**

- **Symptoms**: Severe vomiting, diarrhea (often bloody), loss of appetite, lethargy, fever
- **Prevention**: Vaccination is crucial

**Kennel Cough**

- **Symptoms**: Persistent cough, runny nose, sneezing, loss of appetite
- **Treatment**: Usually resolves with rest, but may need antibiotics

**Ear Infections**

- **Symptoms**: Scratching at ears, head shaking, odor from ears, redness or swelling
- **Prevention**: Regular ear cleaning

**Skin Allergies**

- **Symptoms**: Excessive scratching, red, inflamed skin, hair loss, hot spots
- **Common Causes**: Food allergies, environmental allergens, fleas

#### Cats

**Upper Respiratory Infections**

- **Symptoms**: Sneezing, runny nose, watery eyes, loss of appetite, lethargy
- **Prevention**: Vaccination, reduce stress

**Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease (FLUTD)**

- **Symptoms**: Straining to urinate, blood in urine, frequent urination, crying while urinating
- **Prevention**: Adequate hydration, stress reduction

**Kidney Disease**

- **Symptoms**: Increased thirst, increased urination, weight loss, vomiting, bad breath
- **Common in**: Senior cats

**Diabetes**

- **Symptoms**: Increased thirst and urination, weight loss despite increased appetite, lethargy, vomiting

### 5. Emergency Warning Signs

#### Immediate Veterinary Attention Required

**Difficulty Breathing**

- Rapid or labored breathing
- Blue or pale gums
- Choking sounds
- Open-mouth breathing (cats)

**Severe Vomiting or Diarrhea**

- Blood in vomit or stool
- Persistent for more than 24 hours
- Signs of dehydration

**Urinary Problems** (especially cats)

- Straining to urinate
- No urine production
- Blood in urine

**Loss of Consciousness**

- Seizures
- Collapse
- Unresponsiveness

**Severe Pain**

- Whining or crying
- Reluctance to move
- Aggression when touched

**Trauma**

- Hit by car
- Falls from height
- Animal attacks

**Toxic Ingestion**

- Known ingestion of poison
- Sudden behavior changes
- Excessive drooling

## Age-Specific Care

### Puppies/Kittens

- Frequent feeding schedules
- Socialization and training
- Initial vaccinations
- Parasite prevention
- Regular growth monitoring

### Adults

- Regular exercise
- Annual checkups
- Preventive care
- Weight management
- Dental care

### Seniors

- More frequent veterinary visits (every 6 months)
- Special diets for age-related conditions
- Joint supplements
- Regular monitoring for chronic conditions
- Comfort and quality of life focus

**Emergency:** If your pet is experiencing a medical emergency, contact your veterinarian or an emergency veterinary clinic immediately.
